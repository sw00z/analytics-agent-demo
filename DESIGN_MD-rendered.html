A designer's reference for the Analytics Agent design system — every primitive (colour, type, radius) listed at full specification, paired with a live specimen and the component aliases that compose them. The page itself is rendered in the register it documents.

01. Colors.

A printer's palette.

Warm cream paper, warm near-black ink, a single ink-blue accent stamp, and a four-step chart palette borrowed from press inks. Every neutral is tinted toward hue 75 at chroma 0.005, which is the mechanism behind the cream surface — the page reads as paper because the neutrals are warm.

Primary — the single voice

Ink-Blue Stamp
oklch(0.42 0.1 250) --ink-blue-stamp

The single accent. Ask pill hover fill, masthead mark, user-bubble left rule, focus ring, chart series 1. ≤10% surface coverage at all times.

Ink-Blue Stamp Deep
oklch(0.36 0.12 250) --ink-blue-stamp-deep

Deeper variant for italic emphasis on prose, accent-link hover, and the Q. kicker.

Ink-Blue Stamp Soft
oklch(0.42 0.1 250 / 0.12) --ink-blue-stamp-soft

Base accent at 12% alpha. Backs the resting Ask pill and the focus-within composer ring. Never on a card.

Secondary — destructive ink

Printer's Mark Red
oklch(0.5 0.18 28) --printers-mark-red

Destructive register. Lives on the Note. kicker, the Stop pill border + text, and the rate-limit countdown. Ink, never fill.

Tertiary — chart palette (series 2–5)

Composing Stick
oklch(0.32 0.02 75) --composing-stick

Series 2. Warm near-black at desaturated chroma — the ink the press laid down before the colored runs.

Letterpress Sienna
oklch(0.55 0.13 35) --letterpress-sienna

Series 3. A muted red-orange in the register of an iron-oxide press ink.

Folio Ochre
oklch(0.62 0.12 75) --folio-ochre

Series 4. Same hue family as the cream surface, lifted into a saturated mid-tone. Source hue for the user-bubble's 9% wash.

Compositor Teal
oklch(0.5 0.08 200) --compositor-teal

Series 5. A muted slate-teal — the cool counterweight to sienna and ochre.

Neutral — the cream system

Warm Ash Cream
oklch(0.97 0.005 75) --warm-ash-cream

The surface. Page body, all card and popover backgrounds, the masthead, the composer base.

Warm Ash Cream 2
oklch(0.94 0.005 75) --warm-ash-cream-2

The figure-inset surface. SQL block <pre>, table sticky <thead>, hover row fills. One shade darker on the same paper.

Editor's Ink
oklch(0.22 0.005 75) --editors-ink

The body type colour. Warm near-black at hue 75 — never #000.

Editor's Ink 2
oklch(0.42 0.005 75) --editors-ink-2

Secondary type colour. User-bubble body, italic blockquote, error prose, follow-up chips.

Margin Pencil
oklch(0.58 0.005 75) --margin-pencil

The captioning ink. Mono kickers, axis labels, table headers, captions, the keyboard-hint footer.

Hairline Rule
oklch(0.22 0.005 75 / 0.16) --hairline-rule

The default 1px border. Editor's ink at 16% alpha — the divider that does the work elevation usually does.

Hairline Rule Strong
oklch(0.22 0.005 75 / 0.36) --hairline-rule-strong

Reserved heavier rule for inputs that need to read at distance. Rare.

Named rules

The One Voice Rule.

The accent is the only voice. Ink-Blue Stamp and its two variants are the only saturated colours allowed on chrome — buttons, kickers, focus rings, links, the masthead mark, series 1. Tertiary chart colours are only allowed inside the <figure> boundary. Total accent coverage stays ≤10% of any visible surface.

The Cream Rule.

No #fff, no #000, no purely chromatic neutrals. Every neutral is tinted toward warm hue 75 at chroma 0.005 — the surface leans amber so the page reads as paper, not cold tile.

The Destructive-Is-A-Note Rule.

Printer's Mark Red appears as ink, never as a surface fill. The Note. kicker is red. The Stop button has a red border and red text on a 5%-alpha red wash. There is no destructive card, no red banner, no flooded warning surface.

02. Typography.

Two families, six roles.

Iowan Old Style with a Palatino Linotype / Palatino / Georgia fallback chain carries the editorial register at full reading size (17px / 1.62 leading); IBM Plex Mono carries the structural labels — kickers, axes, code, table headers. Italic emphasis is the deliberate device for warmth and pull; bold colour and heavier weight are reserved for primary action and the accent system.

Named rules

The Single-Family Rule.

Iowan Old Style and IBM Plex Mono are the only families. No third family, no display-only face, no decorative script. The body type is the brand.

The Italic-Emphasis Rule.

Italic is the canonical emphasis device. Bold and colour are reserved for primary action and the accent system — never use <strong> or accent colour as a substitute for italic emphasis on prose.

The Kicker Rule.

Every editorial unit opens with a mono small-caps kicker at 11px / 0.14em with a one-period terminator.

The 60ch Rule.

Body prose never exceeds 60 characters per line, regardless of viewport width. Figures (charts, tables) may exceed the prose column inside their <figure> boundary, but only inside.

03. Radii.

Five steps from sharp to capsule.

A short scale. sm for the composer shell, md for the SQL block, lg for buttons and inputs, xl for cards and dialogs, and full for the Ask / Stop pill which is the only fully-rounded surface in the system.

05. Elevation.

Three shadows, all transient.

The system is flat by default — cards, buttons, inputs, message bubbles carry no shadow at rest. Depth comes from hairline rules and tonal layering between cream and surface-2. Shadow is reserved for transient floating surfaces — dropdown popups, dialogs, chart tooltips.

popup-elevation 0 8px 24px -4px oklch(0.22 0.005 75 / 0.18) Sessions dropdown · Recharts tooltip.
dropdown-md tailwind shadow-md Base UI Menu popup body.
dropdown-lg tailwind shadow-lg Submenu — one step heavier than its parent.

Named rules

The Flat-By-Default Rule.

Resting surfaces are flat. Cards, buttons, inputs, message bubbles, the masthead, the composer — none carry a shadow at rest. If a surface needs depth, it gets a hairline rule and a tonal step on the same paper.

The Popup-Only Shadow Rule.

Shadows are reserved for transient floating surfaces. A persistent shadow on a card is always wrong in this system — the appearance of a shadow is the reader's signal that the surface is temporarily lifted off the page.

The Hairline Rule.

A 1px border at --hairline-rule alpha does the work elevation usually does. Hairline above and below a figure, hairline beneath the masthead, hairline atop the composer. The system reads as paper because the divisions are inked, not lifted.

06. Contracts.

Do's, don'ts.

The values are the easy part. These contracts are what keep the system from drifting once a designer or engineer starts composing tokens into screens.

Do.

  • Open every editorial unit with a mono small-caps kicker — Q., Answer., Note., Working., Stopped., Figure ·, Try.
  • Cap body prose at 60 characters per line. Figures and tables may exceed the prose column inside their <figure> boundary, but only inside.
  • Use italic for emphasis. Italic carries the warmth-and-pull register; bold and accent are reserved for action.
  • Treat error states with the same care as success — italic serif on a hairline-bordered block, a printer's-note kicker, no flooded red surface.
  • Keep accent coverage ≤10% of any visible surface. Never on a card background.
  • Stay flat by default. Hairline rules and a tonal step on the same paper, not a drop shadow.
  • Tint every neutral toward warm hue 75 at chroma 0.005.
  • Keep the chart palette deliberately muted — all five series at lightness ≤0.55. Hue separation does the differentiation work.
  • Keep the type system to two families — Iowan Old Style and IBM Plex Mono.
  • Use the user-bubble's accent left rule only in its full canonical form — paired with the Q. kicker, ochre wash, asymmetric radius, italic 18px body.

Don't.

  • Use border-left or border-right greater than 1px as a colored stripe accent on cards, list items, or alerts. The user-bubble is the only sanctioned exception.
  • Use background-clip: text with gradients. Single solid colour for type, always.
  • Use decorative glassmorphism, blurred backgrounds, or glass cards stacked over a colored backdrop.
  • Add the hero-metric SaaS dashboard cliché — big number, tiny label, gradient accent, supporting stats grid.
  • Render identical card grids with icon-heading-text. The numbered, hairline-divided sample-query list is the editorial replacement.
  • Introduce a third type family — no sans-serif body, no display-only face, no decorative script.
  • Use exclamation points anywhere. The system reports state. It does not perform feelings.
  • Use emoji or chatty assistant tone in error and empty states.
  • Use bold colour or heavier weight as the primary emphasis device on prose. Italic carries weight contrast.
  • Flood the surface with destructive red. Printer's Mark Red is an ink, not a fill.
  • Ship a dark theme. There is one theme — Editorial Paper.
  • Use toy-box pastel rainbow palettes for charts. The 5-step palette stays at lightness ≤0.55.
  • Stack nested cards. Use <figure> with hairline rules and surface-2 insets instead.
